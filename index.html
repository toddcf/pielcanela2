About the Therapist

Brenda Canela, CMT

CAMTC #30305

ABMP #766335

Brenda Canela, CMT studied massage therapy at California Healing Arts College (CHAC) in West Los Angeles, CA. While she excels in deep tissue work, the nurturing factor of Prenatal Massage inspired her to continue coursework through the Institute of Somatic Therapy and earn her certification.

Always looking to further her skill set, she trained with Tina Allen, founder and director of Liddle Kidz Foundation, and became internationally certified as an Infant Massage Teacher. She also received an international certification for Pediatric Massage, and further training for Touch Therapy for Childhood Cancer.

She is CPR, AED, and First Aid certified by The American Heart Association and is an insured member of Associated Bodywork & Massage Professionals (ABMP).

Prior to only working her private practice, Brenda worked at a spa where she was awarded the 2012 Therapist of the Year. She was also a featured Massage Therapist in the spaâ€™s monthly newsletter. Here are some highlights:

What made you decide to get into massage?

When I was first introduced to massage, I didn't know ALL of the health benefits. I simply wanted to relax after a long day at my desk. However, when regular massage sessions relieved me of years of debilitating migraines, I was hooked. I grew interested and appreciated all of the natural benefits in the work. I wanted to be a part of that.

Why do you enjoy doing massage?

Massage is very personal. Each client is a different experience. The client trusts their therapist to cater to their needs, whether it be stress, an injury, or chronic pain. It's a great feeling to know you were able to deliver. A client once sent me a message hours later that I'll never forget. It said, "I'm actually feeling joy about cooking dinner right now. A feeling I haven't had in a very long time. I missed it. I turned on music and danced a while. Thank you for the breath of life." The body heals itself. I only do what I can to facilitate that.

Massage and bodywork are also mentally stimulating for me. I, too, gain peace and balance while focused on the work I do, and that clarity allows for inspiration and enthusiasm. Win–win.

Anything else you would like our readers to know about you?

I've been an actor for about 18 years now. I fell in love with it in high school and was ridiculously fortunate enough to land my first agent then. I received my BA in Theater Arts from Loyola Marymount University and continued to work in film, television, commercials, theater and voice-over.

Objections

— Click for more info —
  • I'm trying to save money

  • Massage is an investment, not an expense. By taking regular care of your body, you are preventing costly breakdowns that will result in health problems, lost exercise, difficulty sleeping, and reduced work efficiency. See the list of benefits above for more info.

  • I don't have time

  • Preemptive body maintenance is less time-consuming (and more enjoyable) than repair. By making time for self-care now, you are saving time and increasing efficiency in the bigger picture.

  • I've never been comfortable with the idea of a massage

  • You will be in a quiet, secure setting of your choosing with a trusted therapist of over six years' experience.

    While most massage and bodywork techniques are traditionally performed with the client unclothed, it is entirely up to you what you want to wear. You should undress to your level of comfort. The therapist will leave the room while you undress, relax onto the table, and cover yourself with a clean sheet or towel.

    You will be properly draped during the entire session. Only the area being worked on will be exposed.

Policy Overview

We understand that unanticipated events happen occasionally in everyone's life. In our desire to be effective and fair to all clients, the following policies are honored:

  • 24 hours advance notice required for cancellation. (10-day advance notice required for group massage cancellation.) Cancellation on shorter notice will result in being billed for the full session.
  • If you do not show up for your appointment, you will be billed for the full session.
  • If you are late for your appointment, your session may be shortened to accommodate others whose appointments follow yours. Depending upon how late you are, the therapist may determine that there is not enough time to begin your session. You will be billed for the full session.
  • Trip Charge
  • Prices subject to change.
